The problem

Managing SCD is exhausting.
The tools haven't kept up.

Families managing sickle cell disease are organized, dedicated, and resourceful. But the information that matters most ends up scattered — and that costs time, clarity, and confidence.

📋

Scattered information

Medications in a notebook. Temperatures in a text thread. Symptoms in your memory. There's no single source of truth — and appointments suffer for it.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Multi-child complexity

Tracking one child is hard. Multiple children — or a child and yourself — is a different challenge entirely. Most apps weren't built for this reality.

🏥

Underprepared appointments

Doctors ask about the last 90 days. Parents piece together what they can remember. The most important conversations happen without the data to support them.

The solution

One platform.
Total confidence.

SickleStrong brings everything together — medications, symptoms, fevers, hydration, and doctor reports — so you walk into every appointment prepared and every night feeling in control.

  • 🌡️
    Emergency Fever Alerts
    Automatic alert at 101.3°F — the SCD-specific threshold. Never second-guess when to go to the ER.
  • 💊
    Medication Tracking
    Log medications with specific times. Mark doses taken. See adherence patterns over time.
  • 🫁
    Pain Body Map
    Front and back body map with 32 zones. Log severity, duration, location, and what helped.
  • 📊
    Doctor-Ready Reports
    90-day summaries with pain patterns, fever history, medication adherence — formatted for appointments.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧
    Family Account & Sharing
    Share a Family Code with parents, grandparents, and caregivers. Cloud sync is rolling out now.
  • 📲
    Install to Your Home Screen
    No app store needed. Add SickleStrong to your phone in one tap — it even works offline.

Founder story

Built by a father who lived the problem.

My name is Jason Lucas. As a father of two sons living with sickle cell disease, I experienced firsthand how difficult it can be to keep track of medications, hydration, symptoms, appointments, and care notes.

Cam is 20. Jaxxon is 7. Different ages, different needs, different stages of independence — but the same disease. I needed one place to manage both of them. Nothing existed.

So I built the tool I wished had always existed. Not a generic health app with a disclaimer at the bottom — a platform that actually understands what 101.3°F means, what a pain crisis looks like, and what an exhausted parent needs at 2am.

SickleStrong is not software. It's a parent's answer to a problem families have been carrying for years.

FAQ

Questions families ask us.

Is SickleStrong really free?
Yes — completely. The first 50 founding families receive free lifetime access. No subscription, no hidden fees, no "premium tier." After 50 families, new users will pay $9.99/month. Founding families never pay. Ever.
Does it work for adults with SCD, or just children?
Both. SickleStrong supports families managing one child, multiple children, adults, or a mix. There's a dedicated self-tracking mode for teens and adults who want to manage their own care. Same features — adapted for your situation.
Do I need to download anything?
No app store needed. SickleStrong runs directly in your phone's browser — just open the link and start. You can also install it to your home screen for a real app icon and offline access, right from your browser.
Is my family's health data private and safe?
Your data is stored locally on your device and never sold to third parties. Family accounts use encrypted storage. We're building toward full Supabase-backed cloud sync with row-level security — meaning your family's data is completely isolated from anyone else's.
Can both parents use the same account?
Yes. The Family Account feature lets you create a Family Code and share it with up to 5 family members — parents, grandparents, caregivers. Everyone can log data and see the same information. Full sync via Supabase is in active development.
What languages will SickleStrong support?
English is live now. French, Spanish, and Portuguese are in development — because 93% of people living with SCD are in Africa, and we're building for the whole global community. Multi-language support launches soon. If you're a native French, Spanish, or Portuguese speaker and want to help us review translations, we'd love your involvement.
